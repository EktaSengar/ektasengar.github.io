Enterprise Licensing for Marketplace

Launching enterprise licensing to unlock large-deal velocity across Atlassian's Marketplace ecosystem.

Context

Atlassian Marketplace had 6K+ apps but no standardized enterprise licensing model. Large enterprise customers (Fortune 500) buying Atlassian products also needed third-party Marketplace apps, but there was no way to license them at enterprise scale — no volume pricing, no centralized procurement, no compliance-ready agreements.

Problem

  • Enterprise customers couldn't procure Marketplace apps through standard enterprise purchasing workflows
  • No volume licensing or centralized billing for app bundles
  • Each of the 2K+ partners had different licensing terms, creating procurement friction
  • Large deals (PwC, Citi, Ford, Honeywell, Accenture) were blocked or delayed by licensing gaps
  • Marketplace apps couldn't participate in enterprise-level seat migrations (Data Center → Cloud)

My Role

Owned end-to-end launch of enterprise licensing for Marketplace apps. Collaborated with engineering, design, finance, sales, and GTM teams, plus 300+ ecosystem developers/partners. Managed the full lifecycle from strategy through launch to post-launch optimization.

Decisions & Trade-offs

Standardized framework vs. per-partner flexibility

Built a standardized licensing framework that all partners could adopt, rather than custom agreements per partner. This reduced procurement friction at the cost of some partner-specific flexibility, but the scale benefits were enormous.

Partner-first GTM

Coordinated with 300+ ecosystem developers on the launch. This was slower than an internal-only launch but critical for ecosystem trust and adoption.

Tied to cloud migration

Scaled Marketplace tiers to 100K+ users and tied enterprise licensing to the Data Center → Cloud migration path, unlocking 1.9M+ Jira seat migrations and boosting adoption by 20%.

Enterprise sales enablement

Built sales enablement materials and pricing tools for Atlassian's enterprise sales team, making Marketplace apps part of the enterprise deal narrative rather than an afterthought.

Metrics & Results

  • $15M+ ACV enabled (PwC, Citi, Ford, Honeywell, Accenture)
  • 600K+ enterprise seats unlocked
  • 300+ ecosystem developers onboarded to the new licensing model
  • Marketplace tiers scaled to 100K+ users
  • 1.9M+ Jira seat migrations unlocked
  • +20% boost in Data Center → Cloud adoption
  • Enterprise Marketplace became part of standard enterprise deal packaging

Learnings

  • Platform strategy is ecosystem strategy. Enterprise licensing wasn't just a product feature — it was an ecosystem play. Getting 300+ partners aligned was harder than building the technology, but it was the difference between a feature and a platform shift.
  • Enterprise deals need packaging, not just features. The licensing model itself was table stakes. What closed deals was packaging: bundled pricing, compliance-ready agreements, and sales enablement that made Marketplace apps part of the enterprise conversation.
  • Migration is a distribution channel. Tying enterprise licensing to the DC→Cloud migration turned a mandatory customer action into a distribution channel for Marketplace apps. 1.9M+ seat migrations became 1.9M+ licensing opportunities.
  • Coordinate early, launch fast. Spending extra time upfront aligning with 300+ partners paid off in launch velocity. Partners who felt included in the process became advocates, not blockers.
